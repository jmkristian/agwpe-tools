This page converts between characters, bytes and Unicode values.

  • The editable boxes in the characters column show the character glyphs.
  • The encoding column identifies a system of converting between characters and bytes.
  • The gray boxes show the Unicode values for the adjacent characters, in hexadecimal notation.
  • The top right hand box shows the UTF-8 encoding of the top left box, also in hexadecimal notation.

The ISO 8859-1 and CP-1252 boxes in the characters column are transcoded from UTF-8. For example, the CP-1252 box shows the result of decoding the UTF-8 bytes as though they represent CP-1252 characters.

The big table provides some sample characters. You can hover your mouse cursor over some of them for an explanation. You can click on them to append characters to the UTF-8 box in the characters column. The other boxes will update accordingly.

You can copy-n-paste between the boxes and other software, as usual. The characters should be transferred correctly, if the software supports Unicode. But don't copy-n-paste from the characters in the big table. This doesn't always work, because your browser copies the wrong character into the clipboard. It's better to click a character in the big table and then copy from the top left box.

You can type into any of the boxes that aren't gray. Then click or tab somewhere else, and the other boxes will update to match. A message pops up when you enter something that can't be converted to another representation.

The 008 and 009 rows in the big table are interesting. Most of these bytes represent different characters in ISO 8859-1 and CP-1252. The characters might look similar, but in the adjacent gray boxes you can see they're different Unicode values.
